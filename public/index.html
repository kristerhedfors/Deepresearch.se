Your data on Deepresearch.se

Your messages are processed by Berget.ai (EU-hosted AI models) to generate answers. When web search is on, search queries derived from your question are sent to Exa to find sources — Exa may retain those queries under its own policy (see the documentation for a two-step workflow around this). Attached photos and documents are sent along with any embedded metadata they carry (e.g. a photo's location, or a Word document's author and edit history) — the attachment card shows a badge whenever that's the case, before you send. A photo's coordinates are also resolved to a place name via OpenStreetMap Nominatim (only the coordinates are sent, nothing else about you or your message) (see the documentation for details).

Your chat history is encrypted in this browser with a key the server never writes down anywhere (see the History panel and the documentation for how that works), and — by default — an encrypted copy is also kept in this site's Cloudflare storage so your history follows your account across devices. Attached files, images included, are stored encrypted the same way; the one readable exception is large documents indexed for search, together with the search index itself (indexing needs readable text). You can switch cloud storage off in Account & usage → Settings: everything is then downloaded to this browser and the cloud copies are deleted — after that, deleting a conversation here or clearing this browser's site data erases it for good, with no copy to recover. Large attached documents are indexed with Berget's embedding model so questions retrieve only the relevant excerpts. Server logs hold technical metadata only (timings, counts), never message content. If your connection drops mid-answer, the finished answer is held server-side for up to 15 minutes so your device can pick it up when it reconnects — it's deleted the moment it's delivered, or when the 15 minutes pass. Cookies: your login session and this notice's acknowledgement. Preferences (model, time target) stay in your browser.
